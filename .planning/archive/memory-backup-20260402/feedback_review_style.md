During /plan-eng-review (2026-03-26), Diego showed a clear pattern: he doesn't just accept framed options — he thinks alongside and proposes alternatives.

**Rule:** When presenting architectural options, leave room for the user to propose something better. Don't assume the 2-3 options you've identified are exhaustive.

**Why:** When asked about the Haiku vs. Sonnet model routing question, Diego interrupted with a better solution neither option had considered — a pre-chat quiz that eliminates the problem entirely. Similarly, he proposed exploring OSS models rather than choosing between the two Claude models. Both ideas were better than the options on the table.

**How to apply:** After presenting options, add "Or is there an approach I haven't considered?" framing when the problem space is genuinely open. If the user interrupts a question mid-flow, treat it as signal they've already solved it differently in their head — ask what they're thinking before re-presenting the original framing.
